You remember August 24, 2006 very well. After
years of discussion, the General Assembly of
the International Astronomical Union decided
to withdraw Pluto's status as the 9th planet of
your solar system. From one day to the next,
only eight planets orbited your sun, Neptune
being the outermost of them.

In the following years a lurid theory was condensed: At the edge of
the solar system must be a hitherto unknown, gigantic celestial
body. The starting point for these assumptions was the data
transmitted by the space probes Voyager 2 and later New
Horizons. Unusual distortions of their measurements and
intermittent interruptions of communication posed a mystery to
the scientists. Initially dismissed as a fantasy, over time many
skeptics were convinced by the conclusive evidence. However, the
data was soon exhausted and no concrete evidence could be
produced.

At the end of their possibilities, the research team around Dr.
Markow started the NAUTILUS project: A manned spaceship was
to be sent out to check the existence of what a new 9th planet
could be. After many setbacks and years of research, the
technology is finally available to complete the mission. All
that remains is the question: With which crew? Volunteer ahead!

You have landed safely on Earth. Your surprising return has
triggered a media response beyond compare. Your
jump through the wormhole spreads like wildfire
on all continents ­ it's the topic of conversation. The
reunion with your families and friends makes you forget
almost all the hardships and after the long time in space
you enjoy the feeling of finally being home again.

The insights and data brought by your journey soon bring
forth spectacular theories. Through the wormhole in
relative proximity to the Earth, the 9th planet can be
reached in no time. A deeper analysis has confirmed that its
surface structure would actually be suitable for
colonization. Your discovery thus opens up unimagined
possibilities. Future astronauts could first be taken to the
9th planet and start their actual mission from a planetary
station built there.

Thus, when space exploration begins at the edge of our
solar system, what was previously considered science
fiction finally seems feasible: interstellar travel. You have
truly helped usher in a new age of space travel!
